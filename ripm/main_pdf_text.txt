Historical Note. The interior-point method was pioneered by Karmarkar [9] and developed in beautiful ways
(including
вҲҡ           [17, 18, 15, 16, 12, 13]). This classical approach appeared to reach its limit of requiring the solution of
  n linear systems until the paper of Cohen, Lee and Song [2] which reduced the complexity of approximate linear
programming to nПү+o(1) log(1/Пө), by introducing the robust central path method. Using further insights, their
algorithm was derandomized by van den Brand [19]. The technique has been extended in subsequent papers to
other optimization problems [14, 7, 23, 8, 20, 6], and has also played a crucial role in faster algorithms for classical
combinatorial optimization problems such as matchings and flows [22, 21, 4].
